For my senior thesis at the University of Michigan, I chose to focus on process rather than outcome, experimenting and playing with ideas to see where they led. Over the course of seven months, I created several small projects, along with countless bits and pieces in between.

To tie everything together, I designed a suite of typefaces inspired by both the discoveries I made along the way. The project explores the tension between the permanence of written language and the uncertainty of human communication, resulting in three typefaces that embrace the ambiguity we often experience when trying to express ourselves.
